## EARTH (grounding)
1. Sleeping grounded (skin connected to earth) normalized overnight cortisol rhythm, reduced pain and stress.
   Ghaly and Teplitz 2004, J. Alternative and Complementary Medicine 10(5):767-776; reviewed in Chevalier et al. 2012, J. Environmental and Public Health, DOI 10.1155/2012/291541.
   HANDLE WITH CARE: n=12, industry linked authors. Say "an early study found". USED: Earth Part 1.
2. Two hours of grounding raised red blood cell zeta potential, reduced clumping (thinner flowing blood).
   Chevalier, Sinatra, Oschman, Delany 2013, JACM 19(2):102-110, DOI 10.1089/acm.2011.0820.
   HANDLE WITH CARE: n=10. "One measured effect", not settled medicine.

## WATER (blue space)
3. SOLID. Review across dozens of studies (18 countries, BlueHealth project): being near, in, on, or under water is consistently linked to lower distress, lower heart and breathing rates, greater wellbeing.
   White, Elliott, Gascon, Roberts, Fleming 2020, Environmental Research 191:110169, DOI 10.1016/j.envres.2020.110169. USED: Water Part 1, trailer.
4. Cold water immersion at 14C raised plasma noradrenaline ~530% and dopamine ~250% (n=6, say "in one study").
   Sramek et al. 2000, European J. Applied Physiology 81(5):436-442, DOI 10.1007/s004210050065. Mood corroboration: Yankouskaya et al. 2023 (PMC9953392).

## FIRE (hearth)
5. SOLID. Watching a fire WITH crackling sound lowered blood pressure in a 226 adult randomized crossover; longer exposure, bigger effect; silent or inverted fire did not calm.
   Lynn 2014, Evolutionary Psychology 12(5), DOI 10.1177/147470491401200509, University of Alabama. USED: Fire Part 1, trailer.
6. The fireside calm as an evolutionary driver of social bonding is Lynn's HYPOTHESIS; frame as "researchers believe".

## AIR (breath)
7. SOLID. Five minutes daily of cyclic sighing (extended exhale) improved mood and lowered breathing rate more than mindfulness meditation over a month; randomized controlled, 100+ participants.
   Balban et al. 2023, Cell Reports Medicine 4(1):100895, DOI 10.1016/j.xcrm.2022.100895, Stanford. USED: trailer; planned Air Part 1.
8. SOLID. Slow breathing at about six breaths per minute raises vagal activity and heart rate variability.
   Laborde et al. 2022, Psychophysiology 59(1):e13952, DOI 10.1111/psyp.13952; dose response Laborde 2021 (PMC8656666).
9. Phytoncides (airborne tree compounds) raised natural killer immune cell activity for days, even diffused in a hotel room.
   Li et al. 2009, Int. J. Immunopathology and Pharmacology 22(4):951-959, DOI 10.1177/039463200902200410.
   HANDLE WITH CARE: n~12-13, single lab. "Studies by forest medicine researchers found".

## SPIRIT, THE SOURCE (NOT an element; awe, meditation, connection to the Source)
10. Awe was the positive emotion most strongly ASSOCIATED with lower IL-6 inflammation markers. Correlational; say "linked to", never "reduces".
    Stellar et al. 2015, Emotion 15(2):129-133, DOI 10.1037/emo0000033, UC Berkeley. USED: trailer; planned Spirit Part 1.
11. Three month intensive meditation retreat: ~one third higher immune cell telomerase vs matched controls. One marker, one time point; "linked to cellular health markers", never "live longer".
    Jacobs et al. 2011, Psychoneuroendocrinology 36(5):664-681, DOI 10.1016/j.psyneuen.2010.09.010, UC Davis Shamatha Project (co author Elizabeth Blackburn, Nobel laureate).
